CHARACTERS

LUKE SKYWALKER


ABOUT


The protagonist of the Star Wars saga, Luke has grown from a farm boy on Tatooine into a determined Jedi Knight. As The Empire Strikes Back begins, Luke is grappling with the loss of his mentor, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and his growing understanding of the Force. After receiving training from Jedi Master Yoda on the remote planet of Dagobah, Luke continues to search for his place in the galaxy and his ultimate destiny. His confrontation with Darth Vader in this film leads to shocking revelations about his family and the fate that awaits him.

Darth Vader


ABOUT


Once a Jedi Knight named Anakin Skywalker, Darth Vader is now the feared Sith Lord who serves the Emperor. In The Empire Strikes Back, Vader is relentlessly pursuing the Rebel Alliance after their defeat at the Battle of Hoth. His obsession with capturing Luke Skywalker intensifies, and he reveals his shocking connection to Luke. Throughout the film, Vader grapples with his dark side while seeking to bring Luke over to the Sith. His dark, imposing presence and his search for power make him one of the greatest villains in cinematic history.

Princess Leia Organa


ABOUT


A strong, courageous leader of the Rebel Alliance, Leia plays a central role in the Resistance against the Empire. As the sister of Luke Skywalker, she remains unaware of their familial bond at this point in the trilogy. Throughout The Empire Strikes Back, Leia demonstrates her bravery and leadership, particularly in the Battle of Hoth and in her unshakable loyalty to her people. She is also embroiled in a complex relationship with Han Solo, with her tough exterior masking deep feelings for him. Leia's resilience and intelligence shine as she continues to fight against the Empire.

Han Solo


ABOUT


A skilled smuggler and reluctant hero, Han Solo is initially in it for the money, but over the course of The Empire Strikes Back, his loyalty to his friends and the Rebel Alliance grows. Han's sharp wit and self-assured demeanor make him a fan favorite, and his romance with Leia deepens, though it's complicated by the pressures of war. His daring escape from the Empire’s clutches in the Millennium Falcon, alongside his loyal Wookiee co-pilot, Chewbacca, showcases his bravery and quick thinking. Han's conflict with Vader culminates in his betrayal by Lando Calrissian, leading to his capture and eventual freezing in carbonite.

Chewbacca


ABOUT


The towering, noble Wookiee from Kashyyyk, Chewbacca is Han Solo’s best friend and co-pilot of the Millennium Falcon. Throughout The Empire Strikes Back, Chewie is a steadfast companion, loyal to his friends and ready to fight to protect them. Chewie’s fierce loyalty to Han is evident when he struggles to rescue him from the clutches of the Empire. Though he does not speak Basic, Chewie's roars and growls communicate his emotions clearly, especially in his determination to help his friends escape.

C-3PO


ABOUT


The protocol droid who provides translations and assistance to the Rebel Alliance, C-3PO is known for his anxious and often comical nature. In The Empire Strikes Back, C-3PO serves as the voice of reason (however reluctantly) and provides moments of levity. His frequent bickering with R2-D2 and his somewhat pessimistic outlook provide humor during the film’s tense moments. Despite his worry and tendency to panic, C-3PO proves to be a reliable ally to the Rebellion, even in the direst of situations.

R2-D2


ABOUT


The astromech droid who is indispensable to the Rebellion, R2-D2 plays a vital role in helping the heroes escape the Empire’s forces. In The Empire Strikes Back, R2’s resourcefulness and bravery continue to aid the heroes, whether it's helping repair the Millennium Falcon or sending critical data to the Rebel Alliance. His close partnership with C-3PO offers moments of humor, but R2’s bravery and intelligence shine through in critical moments. As ever, R2 is more than just a droid—he is a hero in his own right.

Yoda


ABOUT


The ancient and wise Jedi Master, Yoda is one of the last remaining Jedi in the galaxy. On the swampy planet of Dagobah, Yoda takes Luke Skywalker under his wing to train him in the ways of the Force. While initially appearing eccentric and mysterious, Yoda's deep wisdom and mastery of the Force are revealed as he teaches Luke not only about the power of the Force but also about overcoming self-doubt. Yoda's teachings are essential to Luke’s development, though his training is far from easy. Yoda’s faith in Luke's potential plays a critical role in the galaxy’s future.

Lando Calrissian


ABOUT


A smooth-talking gambler and businessman, Lando is introduced as a new character in The Empire Strikes Back. He is the owner of Cloud City, a floating metropolis on the planet Bespin, where Han Solo and the gang hope to find refuge. Lando initially seems to be an ally, but when Darth Vader arrives, he is forced to make a difficult decision. His betrayal of Han Solo is a turning point in the film, but Lando later redeems himself, joining the Rebellion and helping to rescue Han from the clutches of the Empire.

Boba Fett


ABOUT


The feared bounty hunter in The Empire Strikes Back, Boba Fett is hired by the Empire to capture Han Solo, who is on the run after the Battle of Hoth. His cold, efficient demeanor and near-unmatched skill make him one of the most dangerous figures in the galaxy. Boba Fett is hired to track down the Millennium Falcon and eventually captures Han Solo, delivering him to Jabba the Hutt in the film’s final scenes. Despite his limited screen time, Boba Fett’s enigmatic presence leaves a lasting impression, and his reputation as one of the galaxy’s most lethal bounty hunters continues to grow.

Emperor Palpatine


ABOUT


Though physically absent from The Empire Strikes Back, Emperor Palpatine's influence looms large throughout the story. The puppet master behind the Empire, Palpatine represents the ultimate force of darkness in the galaxy. He is revealed to be the true power behind the throne, manipulating Darth Vader and orchestrating the fall of the Jedi. The Emperor's manipulative nature and cold ambition are seen through his attempts to turn Luke Skywalker to the dark side, setting the stage for the eventual confrontation in Return of the Jedi.
